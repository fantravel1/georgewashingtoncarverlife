Where Is George Washington Carver Buried?

Tuskegee University Campus, Alabama

The Burial Site

George Washington Carver is buried on the campus of Tuskegee University in Tuskegee, Alabama. His grave is located next to that of Booker T. Washington, the founder of Tuskegee Institute who recruited Carver in 1896.

The placement of their graves side by side is fitting, as these two men together shaped the institution and left an indelible mark on African American education and agricultural science.

His Gravestone Epitaph

"He could have added fortune to fame, but caring for neither, he found happiness and honor in being helpful to the world."

This epitaph perfectly captures Carver's selfless dedication to helping others over personal gain.

The Funeral

Carver died on January 5, 1943, and was given a funeral befitting his stature as one of America's most beloved scientists. The service was held on the Tuskegee campus where he had spent 47 years of his life.

Mourners came from across the nation to pay their respects. President Franklin D. Roosevelt sent condolences, calling Carver one of the most eminent figures in science.

Visiting the Gravesite

The gravesite is accessible to visitors on the Tuskegee University campus. Nearby, the George Washington Carver Museum houses his laboratory equipment, personal effects, and exhibits about his life and work.

The museum and gravesite together create a pilgrimage destination for those who wish to honor Carver's memory and learn about his extraordinary contributions.
