How Many Schools Are Named After George Washington Carver?

Over 200 schools across America

A Lasting Educational Legacy

Over 200 schools across America are named after George Washington Carver, from elementary schools to high schools. His name adorns educational institutions in almost every state, making him one of the most honored figures in American school naming.

Why Schools Honor Carver

Carver is a natural choice for school namesakes because:

  • Education champion: He devoted his life to teaching and learning
  • Role model: His journey from slavery to scientific achievement inspires students
  • Values: He embodied hard work, curiosity, and service to others
  • STEM pioneer: He represents achievement in science and agriculture
  • Character: His humility and dedication provide moral lessons

Notable Carver Schools

Some prominent schools named for Carver include:

  • George Washington Carver High School - Phoenix, Arizona
  • Carver Center for Arts and Technology - Baltimore, Maryland
  • George Washington Carver Elementary - Chicago, Illinois
  • Carver High School - Atlanta, Georgia
  • George Washington Carver Academy - San Antonio, Texas

Historical Context

Many schools were named after Carver during two periods:

  • 1940s-1950s: Following his death in 1943, during the early civil rights era
  • 1960s-1970s: During integration and Black History recognition efforts

Some schools that originally bore Confederate names have been renamed for Carver in recent years as communities reconsider which historical figures to honor.

Other Things Named for Carver

Beyond schools, Carver's name appears on:

  • Parks and recreation centers
  • Streets and highways
  • Libraries and museums
  • Research centers and agricultural stations
  • A submarine (USS George Washington Carver)
